Background Story

Once a circus clown of great renown, Rainbow Grandma one day found herself aged out of the business. She could not longer do the stunts, gimmicks, and gags that kept her going. Out of pity, her fellow circus members did their best to keep her afloat, but eventually it became clear that she needed to find a new solution. As a last resort, Rainbow Grandma turned to untested cosmetic surgical procedures done in the depths of Central Park's jungles. What emerged was a terrifying creature, part clown, grandma, tower, and cat with pants.

Needless to say, she never had trouble finding work at the circus again. Her circus mates, at first daunted by her appearance, embraced her back with open arms. She continues to travel the greater Los Angeles area performing.

Procedure

I wanted to clean up the draw loop as much as possible, so I wrote a single corpse function and put everything inside. To clean up the corpse function a little more, and to avoid rewriting too many things, I also wrote other functions like fingers and foot. I randomized the hair to give the piece a little movement. I didn't need to randomize the colors because the circles themselves get redrawn every frame anyways.
