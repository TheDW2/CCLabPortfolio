Design Process

When I read the assignment and saw the examples, I immediately began to think about how I could make this feel more "gamelike" and more interactive beyond just the mouse position. The thing that bothered me the most about binding the features to the mouse position was the fact that there was no way to freeze certain features at a certain point to explore other options. I've been playing a bunch of Balatro on the subway recently, and so what I came up with was a slot machine, but for generating face features.

My first challenge was figuring out how much chaos I wanted in my system. I still wanted my face generator to create something facelike in every iteration, no matter how messed up and that meant that I had to set the range of randomness for each facial feature. After that tedious math work, my next challenge was how to freeze each feature. Luckily, I could simply stop the randomization process from drawing every frame by placing it within an if statement checking for a boolean.

Finally, I set up a reset button that allowed you to reset at any point you were unsatisfied or simply finished.

Thoughts on form

I found that the chaotic formation of the face alongside the primitve, childlike shape actually gives it quite a bit of charm.
